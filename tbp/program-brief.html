The problem

Most early-stage founders fail not because their idea was bad — but because they never tested whether it was good. They build before they understand. They pitch before they’ve validated. Start Studio: ID interrupts that pattern.

What it is

Start Studio: ID is TechTown Detroit’s ideation and discovery program for pre-product founders. Working alongside assigned coaches and mentors, founders develop and validate their business model using the Lean Canvas framework.

How advancement works

Founders don’t graduate on a fixed schedule — they graduate when they’re ready. Advancement is evidence-based, not calendar-based. When a founder and their coach determine the canvas is complete and assumptions are tested, the founder defends their model before a panel of investors and ecosystem leaders. Graduation requires a defensible model, not just a finished document. The program is self-paced, but that pace is expected to carry urgency — checkpoint sessions and the coach relationship maintain forward momentum throughout.

Quarterly clock
Intake path

Applications accepted year-round, reviewed quarterly. Founders are interviewed, placed, and onboarded with a coach before entering the progress track.

Always-on clock
Progress path

Biweekly checkpoint sessions drive accountability. Coaches identify bottlenecks, review evidence, and push thinking forward. Advancement is earned — not given.

Monthly clock
Public path

Monthly lectures open to the broader community build a visible culture of entrepreneurship and serve as the program’s primary recruitment funnel.

What founders build
01

A complete Lean Canvas with tested, evidence-backed assumptions

02

Customer discovery evidence validating the problem/solution hypothesis

03

A defensible business model presented before investors and ecosystem leaders

Why it matters

Detroit’s next generation of tech entrepreneurs needs more than ideas — it needs founders who have done the work to understand whether their ideas are real. Start Studio: ID builds that discipline into the earliest stage of the founder journey, before capital is committed and before product is built.
