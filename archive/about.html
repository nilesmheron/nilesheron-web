author's note

Documenting the quiet intervals.
Arranging words to measure the
distance between things.

I am an observer by necessity, a writer by compulsion, and an archivist only recently. For over a decade, I have been writing essays, sketching poems, and taking photographs. Most of this work lived quietly in unorganized hard drives, spiral-bound notebooks, and the margins of books I was supposed to be reading.

This site — The Complete Archive — is an attempt to impose a gentle order on the ephemeral. It is a digital catalog of observations. It is not designed to be a portfolio of triumphs, but rather a transparent record of attempts. Some pieces are polished; others are raw fragments caught mid-thought.

on curation

I believe in the value of negative space. In visual art, it is the area around and between subjects that gives them shape. In writing, it is what is left unsaid that allows the reader to inhabit the work. My curation philosophy mirrors this belief: I aim to present the work plainly, without excessive framing or justification, allowing you to draw your own lines of connection between a photograph taken in Tokyo in 2022 and a poem written in London in 2024.

"The archive is a living organism. It breathes, expands, and occasionally contradicts itself. That is the point."

The pieces here are categorized loosely by medium — poetry, prose, essay, photography — but these boundaries are porous. Often, a photograph is just a poem waiting for syntax, and an essay is simply a photograph rendered in syntax. Feel free to wander.

— mmh.
London, 2024
