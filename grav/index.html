02 — Master Frame

Luther is the Choice between Light and Dark.

03 — Definition

Lutheroic / adj.

etymology: Lutheran

  1. doing the right thing when no one is watching.
  2. the willingness to respond to be the "someone" the world needs.
  3. the choice to carry the darkness, so others don't have to.
  4. doing all of the work, and paying all of the costs, required of heroes, while rejecting the title, the recognition. Imposter Syndrome for Heroes.
04 — What This Platform Is

Luther is the wall between good and evil — committed to protecting the good by exposing himself to the evil he must repel and defeat. He is made of the same stuff as the legion villain, but chooses to bear it so as to protect others from it.

Luther is a choice and a question. He proves you can choose good. He asks whether or not we have done it.

18 — Appendix / Art Direction

Appendix — Art Direction

Cover Blend v0.1

Staging. Rain-slicked cobblestone intersection. East End sign left, City West sign right. Idris center-frame but slightly offset — caught mid-step, not posed.

Pose. Looking back over his shoulder toward camera. Reads as "you caught him in the middle of choosing."

Prop. Flashlight in hand, beam angled down to the cobblestones. Beam illuminates graffiti tagged on the wet stone.

Graffiti. "CHOOSE" (primary) or "?" (alternate). A's slug asks "What do you choose when it's dark?" — the graffiti is the imperative answer.

Crowd. Male-majority (brief-compliant), with selected women present to prevent monolithic read. Mix of trench coats, suits, colorful hooded raincoats (yellow/red/blue) for visual rhythm. Facing away, toward both ends of the street — both directions of the fork.

Lighting. Blue-hour dusk. Gaslamps lit. Light rain. Wet surfaces catching lamplight.

Net effect. Luther at the fork, with the instrument of his choice (flashlight), illuminating the question on the ground (graffiti), inside a crowd of men moving both directions without knowing they're being asked anything. The "lonely magsafe flashlight in a sea of darkness" metaphor rendered as a composition.

Source Options

Option 1 — Crossroads, Male Crowd

Idris between East End / City West signs. Crowd of men in varied coats, facing away. Turns back toward camera. Literal "fork in the road." Brief-compliant.

Option 2 — Hidden Witness

Idris in shadow behind a corner with flashlight, watching colorful hooded men in rain. Heavy gaslamp mood. Surveillance-coded. Self-illumination.

Option 3 — Center, Surrounded

Idris facing camera, flashlight beam on tagged cobblestones. Crowd of colorful hooded men facing inward at him. Most iconic composition — singular, centered.

Option 4 — Mixed Crowd

Composition similar to Option 1, but crowd includes women and broader demographic mix. Flashlight on graffiti. Signage present.
