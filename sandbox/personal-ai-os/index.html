OneBite

A free tool

Figure out what AI could actually be doing for you.

Sometimes AI can be scary. Mostly that’s because people don’t understand it — not because it’s complicated, but because no one has taken the time to explain what it can do for them specifically.

This tool isn’t owned by a company and has no preference toward any particular solution. It’s going to ask you some questions about how you work, what’s breaking down, and what’s held you back from AI so far. Then it’ll give you an honest assessment: what already exists that could help you, what could be built for you specifically, and where to start.

How it works

1
A short conversation. An AI asks you about what you do, the tools you use, where things fall apart, and what’s stopped you from doing more with AI.
2
A real assessment. What we heard, and what we suggest — specific tools, a clear first move, and a step-by-step setup guide tailored to your situation.
3
Something you can act on. Download your setup guide and bring it into Claude or any AI assistant to start building.

Example output

Jordan is a founder constantly creating documents — investor updates, partnership briefs, staff communications — but her process is fragmented and she doesn’t trust putting sensitive deal info into AI tools.

AI AssessmentJordan
What we heard
You’re running your document workflow across Google Docs, email drafts, and your own memory. Every document starts from scratch with no shared context. The privacy concern is legitimate — but it’s also kept you from using AI for things where it would be completely safe.
What we suggest
Claude Projects — A private workspace where Claude uses only your documents. Nothing leaves your project. Start here.

Notion AI — If you move to Notion, its AI layer drafts and summarizes inside your workspace. Real switching cost, but high payoff.

The setup guide (in your .md download) walks you through three levels: quick wins this week, off-the-shelf stack over a weekend, and a fully connected system.

Takes about 10 minutes. Your conversation isn’t stored by default.
