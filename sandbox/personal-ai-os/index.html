Personal AI OS

A free tool

Figure out what AI could actually be doing for you.

Sometimes AI can be scary. Mostly that's because people don't understand it — not because it's complicated, but because no one has taken the time to explain what it can do for them specifically.

This tool isn't owned by a company and has no preference toward any particular solution. It's going to ask you some questions about how you work, what's breaking down, and what's held you back from AI so far. Then it'll give you an honest assessment: what already exists that could help you, what could be built for you specifically, and where to start.

How it works

1
A short conversation. An AI asks you about what you do, the tools you use, where things fall apart, and what's stopped you from doing more with AI. It follows your lead — if you want to explore, it'll stay with you.
2
A real assessment. Not generic advice. Based on what you actually shared: a read of your current setup, specific tools that could help, what a custom build would look like, and where to start given your actual situation.
3
Something you can act on. The goal isn't to impress you with AI. It's to give you one clear next move that makes sense for where you are right now.

Where you are now
You're running your document workflow across Google Docs, email drafts, and your own memory — which works until it doesn't. The real gap isn't that you can't write; it's that every document starts from scratch with no shared context. The privacy concern is legitimate: you're right not to paste sensitive cap table or deal structure information into a general-purpose chatbot. But that concern has also kept you from using AI for anything, including the parts where it would be completely safe and genuinely useful.
What already exists that could help
Claude Projects — A private workspace where you upload your own documents and Claude uses only that context to help you write. Nothing leaves your project. Directly addresses the privacy concern.

Notion AI — If you're willing to move document management into Notion, its AI layer can draft and summarize directly inside your workspace. The trade-off: real switching cost.

A private GitHub repo — Keeping master documents in a version-controlled private repo means you always have a clean, current source to pull from. Pairs well with Claude.
Where to start
Start with Claude Projects this week, not a build. Create a Project, upload your three most-used documents, and use it to draft the next thing you have to write. Trust first, complexity second.
If you build this out
In twelve months, drafting a new investor update or partnership brief goes from a half-day task to thirty minutes. You're iterating from a living, accurate source of truth rather than starting from scratch every time.

Takes about 10 minutes. Your conversation isn't stored by default.
