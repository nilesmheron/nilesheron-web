Personal AI OS

A free tool

Figure out what AI could actually be doing for you.

Sometimes AI can be scary. Mostly that's because people don't understand it — not because it's complicated, but because no one has taken the time to explain what it can do for them specifically.

This tool isn't owned by a company and has no preference toward any particular solution. It's going to ask you some questions about how you work, what's breaking down, and what's held you back from AI so far. Then it'll give you an honest assessment: what already exists that could help you, what could be built for you specifically, and where to start.

How it works

1
A short conversation. An AI asks you about what you do, the tools you use, where things fall apart, and what's stopped you from doing more with AI. It follows your lead — if you want to explore, it'll stay with you.
2
A real assessment. Not generic advice. Based on what you actually shared: a read of your current setup, specific tools that could help, what a custom build would look like, and where to start given your actual situation.
3
Something you can act on. The goal isn't to impress you with AI. It's to give you one clear next move that makes sense for where you are right now.

Example output

Jordan is a founder building a new infrastructure venture. She's constantly creating documents — investor updates, partnership briefs, staff communications — but her process is fragmented, slow, and she doesn't trust putting sensitive deal information into AI tools. Here's what her assessment looked like.

AI Assessment Jordan
Where you are now
You're running your document workflow across Google Docs, email drafts, and your own memory — which works until it doesn't. The real gap isn't that you can't write; it's that every document starts from scratch with no shared context, which means you're re-explaining your venture's position, tone, and strategy every time you sit down to write. The privacy concern is legitimate: you're right not to paste sensitive cap table or deal structure information into a general-purpose chatbot. But that concern has also kept you from using AI for anything, including the parts where it would be completely safe and genuinely useful.
What already exists that could help
Claude Projects — Anthropic's Projects feature lets you create a private workspace where you upload your own documents (decks, briefs, strategy memos) and Claude uses only that context to help you write. Nothing leaves your project. This directly addresses the privacy concern while giving you a document partner that already knows your voice and your venture's position. The limitation: it requires some upfront work to populate the project with your source materials.

Notion AI — If you're willing to move your document management into Notion, its AI layer can draft, summarize, and iterate directly inside your workspace with your existing pages as context. Good for teams. The trade-off: it requires migrating your current setup, which has real switching cost.

A private GitHub repo as document source of truth — Not a tool per se, but a pattern: keeping your master documents (investor narrative, company overview, key frameworks) in a version-controlled private repo means you always have a clean, current source to pull from when drafting anything new. Pairs well with Claude.
What a bespoke build could look like
A lightweight personal document OS: a private GitHub repo holding your master context files (company overview, investor narrative, partner brief templates, key talking points), connected to Claude via a simple interface where you specify the audience and document type and get a draft back in your voice using only your materials. No sensitive data goes anywhere you haven't chosen. Build cost: low. Most of this can be set up in a weekend with no code using Claude Projects plus a disciplined folder structure. The full version — a custom web interface with a private API proxy — adds maybe two to three days of work and gives you a shareable link for collaborators.
Where to start
Start with Claude Projects this week, not a build. Create a Project, upload your three most-used documents (company overview, a recent investor update, and one partnership brief), and use it to draft the next thing you have to write. The point isn't the output — it's proving to yourself that AI can work with your actual materials and stay private. Once you've seen that work twice, you'll have a much clearer picture of what's worth building versus what already exists. Trust first, complexity second.
If you build this out
In twelve months, drafting a new investor update or partnership brief goes from a half-day task to a thirty-minute one. You have a versioned library of your own materials that any collaborator can query with the right permissions. You're not starting from scratch every time — you're iterating from a living, accurate source of truth that reflects where the venture actually is. The time you save on document creation goes back into the conversations and decisions that actually move things forward.

Takes about 10 minutes. Your conversation isn't stored.
